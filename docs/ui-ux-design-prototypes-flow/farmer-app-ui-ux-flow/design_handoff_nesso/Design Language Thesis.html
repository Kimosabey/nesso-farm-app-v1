The thesis · one synthesis, not one pillar
“Cultivated Clarity”

Editorial confidence with field-grade legibility. Big, quiet type and a bento rhythm carry the data; glass appears only on hero cards, the topbar, and modals — never on tables. Warmth comes from the brand's botanical palette and considered motion, not decoration. The test for every pixel: would it read at arm's length, in sunlight, through gloves — and would a designer in Berlin screenshot it?

§13 · The seven open questions

Seven decisions, proposed.

Each is a recommendation with reasoning — not a menu. Disagree with any and I'll pivot before the system hardens. The visuals are live: toggle the theme up top to see both at once.

01 / Mood pillar

Editorial, glass, bento, or soft-skeuo?

→ A synthesis: editorial + bento, glass as seasoning

None of the four alone clears the bar. I propose “Cultivated Clarity”: editorial typography and a bento dashboard rhythm as the backbone, with glass used surgically — topbar, hero/login cards, modals — and never on data surfaces where it kills contrast outdoors.

Soft-skeuo and neumorphism are out: they fail contrast and the sunlight-glove test. The result reads as premium and calm, but every KPI stays glanceable.

02 / Default theme

Dark-default marketing, or open in light?

→ Light by default in-app · dark is the showpiece

The primary audience is field officers in direct sunlight — light themes with high-contrast porcelain backgrounds simply win outdoors, and the original PRD's dark-glass instinct fights glare. So the app opens in light, follows prefers-color-scheme, and remembers the choice.

Dark stays genuinely first-class — audited on every screen — and is where the glass glow and the marketing/QR-portal hero shine. Both designed twice, never “dark later.”

03 / Splash mascot

Abstract logomark, or an agri illustration?

→ Abstract logomark paint-in (no character)

I recommend the logomark animating in — the clover-of-leaves blooms petal-by-petal and the golden essence-drop swells at center — rather than a sprout mascot. It's faster (≤ 80 KB Lottie / Skia), brand-true, and ages better than a cartoon character.

It also doubles as the offline→online “synced” flourish and pull-to-refresh spinner, so one motion idea earns its keep across the app. Press play →

04 / Logo treatment

Keep the JPEG, or evolve to vector?

→ Vectorize · knock out the white · build a set

The supplied JPEG is a raster on a white box — fine on a porcelain login card, but it can't sit on the green splash without an ugly square. I propose we trace it to clean SVG, knock out the background, and ship a small logo set: full lockup, standalone mark (the clover+drop) for app icons & topbars, a monochrome for stamps, and a reverse/white for green surfaces.

Same identity — just production-grade and theme-safe. The wordmark stays as drawn.

05 / Illustration style

Flat-geometric, isometric, or hand-drawn?

→ Flat-geometric · soft brand gradients · no outlines

Per §11.8 I'll commit to flat-geometric empty-state illustrations: simple shapes, soft brand-color gradients, subtle highlights, no outlines, one accent pop. They're tiny (inline SVG, 160×120), recolor instantly across both themes, and never compete with text.

Isometric reads dated fast and bloats; hand-drawn fights the system's precision. Geometry keeps it calm, on-brand, and cheap to ship in volume.

06 / Chart aesthetic

A signature chart look, or convention-safe?

→ A restrained signature: rounded caps, soft fills, no chartjunk

I propose a quiet signature: rounded bar caps, soft gradient area fills, no axis lines (a faint dotted baseline instead), generous gaps, and the brand series order. It looks bespoke without sacrificing readability.

Crucially it stays color-blind safe — every series carries a shape/label, never color alone — and degrades to flat fills under reduced-data or print. Bespoke, but legible at a glance.

07 / Signature interaction

A cursor / focus signature on web?

→ Magnetic primary CTAs + a golden hover halo

The web signature is a magnetic primary CTA: the button gently leans toward the cursor (≤ 6px) with a soft golden halo on hover. It's memorable, not gimmicky — gated to hover:hover devices and disabled under prefers-reduced-motion.

Everywhere else the signature is discipline: a crisp 2px focus ring in brand green with 2px offset, never removed. Delight on pointer, rock-solid for keyboard. Hover the button →
